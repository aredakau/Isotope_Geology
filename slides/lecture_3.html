Factors Controlling Natural Abundance of Isotopes

  • Nuclear Stability: Stable isotopes are more abundant.
  • Production Mechanisms: the production mechanisms influence isotopes relative abundances in nature (i.e., Stellar Production, Lighter isotopes are often more abundant than heavier isotopes).
  • Decay Rates: Isotopes with shorter half-lives decay faster and they are less aboundance in nature.
  • Cosmic and Geological Processes: Alterations from events and processes make some isotopes less aboundace compare to others..
  • Chemical and Physical Processes: Isotopic fractionation affacts the abundance of isotopes.
  • Atomic Number: Affects stability and abundance; higher atomic numbers often lead to more radioactive isotopes.

Nuclear Stability

  • Binding Energy: Energy required to hold nucleons together, higher binding energies increase stability.
  • Neutron-to-Proton Ratio: Balance between protons and neutrons affects stability.
  • Magic Numbers: 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126.
